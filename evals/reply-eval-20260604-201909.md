## TL;DR

This reference account is a textbook **AI-reply-guy**: concede-then-pivot ("well actually") formula, one recycled thesis hammered across unrelated threads, zero personal specificity, occasional fabricated stats, and a schizophrenic voice (207-char systems-design paragraphs next to "cfbr"/"Insta"/"8 min"). A full day of 83 replies earned a **median of 0 likes** and **50 likes total**. It is a clean empirical case for everything our voice doc forbids — and a checklist of failure modes to inoculate our prompt against.

## Targeting findings (biggest lever — not a prompt fix)

Capture rate = reply views ÷ original-post views.

**Replying to whales is a trap** (oView ≥ 2,000):

| Original views | Reply views | Reply likes | Capture |
|---|---|---|---|
| 70,000 | 30 | 0 | 0.04% |
| 51,000 | 86 | 3 | 0.17% |
| 11,000 | 34 | 0 | 0.31% |
| 6,800 | 21 | 1 | 0.31% |
| 6,778 | 27 | 0 | 0.40% |
| 4,939 | 39 | 2 | 0.79% |

**Where the ROI actually lives** — mid-size tweets (≈1k–5k views), replied to early:
- 1,541-view tweet → **114 reply views, 2 likes** (7.4% capture — best of day)
- 2,836-view tweet → 70 reply views
- 4,939-view tweet → 39 reply views, 2 likes

**Rule of thumb from this data: skip sub-100-view tweets (ceiling ~10 views) and 20k+ whales (buried at <0.2%). Spend the reply budget on the 1k–8k band, within ~10 min of posting.** Median reply here went out 17 min after the original; 29/83 within 10 min.

## Prompt-improvement candidates (for `prompt.ts` / `reply prompt.md`)

Derived from this eval. Treat as hypotheses to validate against future scrapes before committing.

1. **Make specificity a hard gate in the reply path** — every reply must contain at least one of: a lived detail from the two laboratories, a real number from his life, or a named tool/scene. (Currently §6 says "texture, not a checklist" — too soft for replies.)
2. **Add an explicit forbidden-openers list for replies:** "True, but", "Agree, but", "Fair point, but", "Maybe. But", "Sounds like", "Exactly.", "I've seen too many", "Yeah, and that's exactly why". First 7 words must be a standalone claim, never a concession-pivot.
3. **Ban the recycled AI-replacement thesis** — add "edge cases / judgment / messy human environments / long tail" to the slop list when used as the whole point. Force a laboratory-specific angle instead.
4. **Hard rule: never fabricate a number.** Real numbers from his life only.
5. **Default replies to ONE punchy line; two only if the angle earns it.** Length ≠ likes here; the mandated multi-proposition block reads like a mini-essay under someone's tweet.
6. **Add a "Punchline"/humor reply register** to §8. Highest like-rate replies in this corpus are dry jokes; the prompt currently has Plain/Spicy/Reflective but nothing funny.
7. **Targeting (pipeline, not prompt):** bias reply selection toward 1k–8k-view tweets within ~10 min of posting. Biggest single lever; no wording change competes with it.

## Caveats

- Single morning, single account — directional, not statistical. Re-check patterns as the corpus grows.
- Views/likes are low-N; like-rate percentages are noisy below ~30 views.
- Scraping may have flattened multi-line reply formatting into single blocks, so the "single block vs propositions" read is uncertain.
- This is **not** our account, so the persona mismatch is expected — it's the value of the sample (a clean negative example), not a defect in our generator.
