### 1.2 The virality engine — 6 components (data-backed, not vibes)

1. **Question with a withheld answer.** You gave zero opinion. Every reader with an opinion became a co-author. 2,886 people wrote your content for you. Compare: your posts that *answer* their own question average 200–1,500 views.
2. **Era-contrast frame ("2010s → 2030s").** A template everyone recognizes + a blank everyone can fill. It compresses a decade of AI anxiety into 2 lines. The debate was pre-loaded — you just handed out the microphone.
3. **Collective anxiety as fuel.** "Is coding still the golden advice?" is the single most emotionally charged question in your niche in mid-2026. Timeliness × emotion = replies.
4. **Zero friction.** 91 characters, 2-second read, no link (also: no $0.20 URL surcharge), no image, no thread. Nothing to consume before reacting.
5. **Polarizing reply ecosystem.** "Learn to own nothing and be happy", "Actually learn to code", "Learn distribution" — meme-able replies got quote-tweeted (506×), each quote re-injecting the original into fresh timelines. A feedback loop you can't buy.
6. **You worked the thread.** 59 replies posted Jul 17–18. Your own replies *inside* the thread earned 27k / 23k / 21k / 12k views each — more than any original you've written except the hit itself. Replying to commenters is not politeness; it's throughput.
