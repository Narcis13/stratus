## Part 1 — Diagnosis: why the profile leaks

A profile converts when a stranger can answer three questions in 5 seconds: **Who is this? Why should I care? What do I get if I follow?** Right now each element answers a *different* question:

| Element | Current | Problem |
|---|---|---|
| Banner | Dark doodle, "**Just started**" | Directly contradicts your #1 asset ("30 years"). Prime real estate saying "nothing to see yet" |
| Avatar | Pirate portrait | Actually your best asset — distinctive, human, memorable. But nothing else references it, so it reads as random costume |
| Name | "Narcissus" | No descriptor. In replies (where most strangers meet you) it says nothing |
| Bio | 30 yrs / compounding loops / don't quit / fewcookies.com | Four lines, four directions. "Closing compounding loops" is insider-speak. No follow promise |
| Link | ship-or-die.com mission page | Third-party page, not your product |
| Pinned | Jun 16 "submitted FewCookies for review" | A month-old status update. No story, no numbers, no reason to follow |

**Five stories compete: Greek myth, pirate, "just started" rookie, 30-year veteran, FewCookies.** The viral visitor met a punchy question-asker, clicked through, and found none of that voice. That's the leak.

The fix is not "better lines." It's **one story told five times.** And the stance pivot sharpens what that one story is: the earlier draft of this playbook made the story "watch my number go up" (a % in the name field, a progress bar in the banner, a filling ring on the avatar). That version now **contradicts the stance you just took in public.** A profile wearing three follower-count meters while the pinned words say "people over numbers" is the same disease as the "Just started" banner — surfaces arguing with each other. The meters come off; the people go on.

## Part 2 — The identity core (decide once, reuse everywhere)

Your unfair differentiator in the build-in-public ocean (which is 95% 20-somethings): **age + receipts + the night-shift grind — and a crew, not an audience.** Nobody can copy "51, 30 years of code, ships with AI after the day job." And the pirate visual gives it a device the timeline can *see*: pirates ship, and pirates have crews. **Ship or die** is already your phrase.

> **Positioning statement (internal compass, never posted verbatim):**
> *The 51-year-old code pirate. 30 years of experience, shipping his first public SaaS at night with AI — real numbers, no hype. He knows every member of his crew by name (he built a tool for it), and the follower count is just the wake behind the boat.*

**Identity laws (the memorability contract):**
1. **The face never changes.** Same portrait, same crop, forever. The avatar is how 48px strangers recognize you in the reply ocean.
2. **One color pair everywhere:** deep navy + gold (they're already in the portrait). Banner, cards, progress visuals — same two colors.
3. **One catchphrase:** "Ship or die." Use as signature on weekly recap posts only (weekly = signature; daily = spam).
4. **One stance: people first — numbers follow.** Public numbers appear only as gratitude and only as *we* ("1,000 of us"), never as a target being chased. No live meters on the profile: no % in the name, no progress bar, no filling ring. The scoreboard lives in stratus (private instrument panel); the profile shows the crew. Milestone counts ("1,000+ of us") update at milestones, not weekly.
5. **Stratus is shown, never pitched.** It appears only attached to a real people-moment it produced ("stratus reminded me that…") — see Part 5 for the teasing rules.

## Part 5 — Teasing stratus without pitching it

The product decision is 2–3 months out; the demand test starts now. Rules of the tease:

1. **Show the output, never the tool.** "stratus reminded me @x was the first person to ever reply to me — that was 8 weeks ago today" beats any feature description. The tool appears only as the *explanation* for an unusually personal public moment.
2. **At most one explicit mention per week** (the Sunday muster is the natural slot). The bio's single mention does the ambient work the rest of the time.
3. **Never "coming soon," never a waitlist, never a screenshot-dump.** One Studio-branded screenshot of a genuinely interesting surface (a dossier, the digest) is allowed when it *is* the story — navy/gold, watermark on, so even the tease is on-brand.
4. **Log the demand signals.** Every "what's stratus?" / "I need this" reply or DM: note it on the person's dossier and tag them + the idea into a `#stratus` channel (C8 — create it with keywords `stratus, crm, remember, circles`). In 2–3 months the sell/don't-sell decision reads straight out of that channel: how many distinct people asked, unprompted, and who they are.
5. **The dogfood is the marketing.** Being visibly the person who never forgets a name IS the stratus pitch. If that reputation doesn't form, the product isn't ready either — same test, zero extra work.
